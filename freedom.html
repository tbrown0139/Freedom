Constitutional Rights

Understanding and protecting the fundamental rights guaranteed by the Constitution and its Amendments

First Amendment Rights

Freedom of speech, religion, press, and assembly, and the right to petition the government for redress of grievances.

Second Amendment

The right of the people to keep and bear arms, ensuring the security of a free state through a well-regulated militia.

Due Process

Protection against unreasonable searches, self-incrimination, and the right to fair trial as guaranteed by the 4th, 5th, and 6th Amendments.

States' Rights

Powers not delegated to the federal government are reserved to the states or to the people, as established by the 10th Amendment.

Preserving Liberty

Your constitutional rights require constant vigilance and active participation to preserve. Understanding and exercising these rights is essential to maintaining the freedoms our founders established.

  • Know and understand your constitutional rights
  • Exercise your rights responsibly and regularly
  • Stay informed about legislation affecting your rights
  • Engage in civil discourse to protect liberty
