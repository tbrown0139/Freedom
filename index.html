Why Education Matters

A large share of Americans have never read the Constitution. Government benefits when citizens don't know their rights.

The Problem

Most people today cannot tell you what the First Amendment protects, what the Tenth Amendment limits, or what due process means. When citizens don't know what the Constitution says, they cannot recognize when government oversteps. That ignorance is used to government's advantage.

The Bill of Rights

The first ten amendments guarantee your freedoms. Know them:

  • Freedom of speech, religion, press, and assembly
  • Right to keep and bear arms
  • Protection against unreasonable searches
  • Rights of the accused in criminal cases
  • Powers reserved to the states and the people

What You Can Do

Defending the Constitution starts with reading it:

  • Read the Constitution and Bill of Rights for yourself
  • Learn what each amendment protects
  • Pay attention when new laws threaten your rights
  • Share what you learn with family and friends
  • Hold elected officials to constitutional limits

Protect What Was Given to You

The Constitution and Bill of Rights were written to limit government and protect the people. But a document only protects those who know it exists and demand that it be followed.

There will come a time when the Constitution hangs by a brittle thread — ready to fall unless citizens who understand it stand up to defend it. We promote the Constitution, educate on the rights it guarantees, and call Americans to protect liberty from government overreach and outside influence.

  • Promote the Constitution and what it actually says
  • Educate citizens on the Bill of Rights
  • Defend individual rights against government overreach
  • Get people involved before it's too late
